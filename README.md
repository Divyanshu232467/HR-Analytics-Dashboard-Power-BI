# HR Analytics Dashboard | Power BI Project

### **Project Overview**
This project analyzes HR data for **1,470 employees** to identify workforce trends and key factors behind employee attrition. The dashboard provides a visual breakdown of why employees leave the company, focusing on demographics, job roles, and salary distributions.

---

###  Key Metrics (KPIs)
* **Total Employees:** 1,470
* **Attrition Count:** 237 
* **Attrition Rate:** 16.12% 
* **Average Age:** 36.92 Years 
* **Average Salary:** 6.50K 
* **Average Years at Company:** 7.01 Years 

---

###  Major Insights
***High Attrition Roles:** Laboratory Technicians (62)and Sales Executives (57)have the highest turnover counts.
* **Age Group Trends:** Employees aged **26-35** are the most likely to leave, accounting for 116 attrition cases.
* **Education Factor:** The highest attrition comes from employees with **Life Sciences (38%)** and **Medical (27%)**backgrounds.
* **Gender Breakdown:** Attrition is significantly higher among **Males (143)** compared to **Females (80)**.
* **Salary Impact:** A large portion of attrition occurs in the **Upto 5k** salary slab.

---

###  Technical Stack
* **Tool:** Power BI Desktop 
* **Data Cleaning:** Power Query (ETL process)
* **Analysis:** DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) for measures like Attrition Rate and Salary Slabs
* **Visuals:** Bar Charts, Donut Charts, Tree Maps, and KPI Cards

---

###  How to Explore
1. **Download:** Get the `.pbix` file from this repository.
2. **Open:** Use **Power BI Desktop** to view the interactive report.
3. **Interact:** Use the built-in filters to analyze data by Department, Job Role, or Education.
